Privacy and Cookies Policy

Introduction

Whilst using this CBT package, the pages you see, along with a short text file called a 'cookie', are downloaded to your computer (or simply 'loaded' if you are accessing them from a local copy on your computer). Many websites do this, because cookies enable web publishers to do useful things like find out whether the computer (and probably its user) has visited certain pages before. This is done on a repeat visit by checking to see, and finding, the cookie left there on the last visit.

Information supplied by cookies can help us to provide you with a better online user experience by providing additional features in the CBT package. For example: a cookie is used to record the last viewed page so when you close your browser and return, you can have the option of going straight to where you left off.

What is a cookie?

A cookie is a small amount of data, which often includes an anonymous unique identifier that is sent to your browser from the place where the webpages are loaded from and stored on your computer's hard drive. Each website can send its own cookie to your browser if your browser's preferences allow it, but (to protect your privacy) your browser only permits a web site to access the cookies it has already sent to you, not the cookies sent to you by other sites.

Many sites do this whenever a user visits their website in order to track online traffic flows.

Users have the opportunity to set their computers to accept all cookies, to notify them when a cookie is issued, or not to receive cookies at any time. The last of these, of course, means that certain personalised services cannot then be provided to that user and accordingly you may not be able to take full advantage of certain features. Each browser is different, so check the "Help" menu of your browser to learn how to change your cookie preferences.

If you have set your computer to reject cookies you can still use this CBT package without any problems. For further information on cookies please visit http://www.aboutcookies.org/.

List of cookies used in this CBT package

This is a list of the cookies that this sets, and what each is used for:

Cookie name Purpose
lastpage When ever you visit a CBT package course content page, the page address you have visited is stored in this cookie. If you close your browser and return, on the CBT package front page your will see an option to "Return to Last Viewed Page".
themeviewtype Used to store the last view type for presenting the modules contained in a theme, i.e. either the "Map" view or the "Index" view. Each time you view the modules for a theme it will be in this last view type format.
